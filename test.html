'The Life and Times of Maciej'

I was born in 1986 in Gliwice, Poland. My family and I moved to Chicago on September 13th, 1987. We lived in the Portage Park area for a few years before moving out to the suburbs. I was fortunate to get a good education, including the (at the time) dreadful Saturday's of Polish school. More recently, I moved to Bridgeport after I was accepted into the graduate program at IIT.

Music Studies

I didn't study anything related to computer technology or computer programming until about two years before I decided to apply to IIT. My undergraduate years were spent studying music composition at Columbia College Chicago. I was fortunate to have enjoyed a lot of unique and rare experiences that I would never have been a part of otherwise.

At the controls (Sun Studios)

I spent a lot of time writing music that I thought was challenging and pretty interesting. My classmates and I were very grateful to have our compositions performed by professional musicians.

IIT and Beyond

My plan for the time I have left at IIT is to do everything and anything I can to become a better Android Developer. I was recently very blessed to have been accepted into IIT's new mentorship program. I am being guided down a path towards hard work and a career I can value by an IIT alumnus. I have been incredibly blessed to have this opportunity. I'm currently working on an app that is meant to help users learn and study Chinese verbs. I'm trying to challenge myself and make the app stand out by using pictures to show the meaning of the verbs instead of using translated English text.

Wish I Had More Time For

Even before I knew much about programming, I thought that being a firmware developer was where it was at in the tech industry. I went to a convention over the summer and attended an "Arduino for Newbies" workshop and I was hooked. My career plan is to get into the indusrtry as an Android Developer and then start moving towards Embedded Android or any embedded systems engineering opportunities that might come my way. Until then, I'll be dreaming of wires...
